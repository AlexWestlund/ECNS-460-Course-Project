Visualizations

We can see that there are three different populations of products within THC. There are potentially two populations for CBD distributions, but less different from each other than the THC populations. By splitting up among product types, we can try to get at each distribution.

Now looking at how many data entries we have for product type, we can see that there are 4 dominating categories. Edibles, Concentrate, Vape, and Flower combined make up the vast majority of data points. Moving forward we will keep items only from these 4 categories to minimize contributions from low volume products.

Next we see a chart of the proportion of all chemical compounds present in our dataset. Noticing some columns have very little representation in any product type.

Now looking at each independent compound in Flower and the relative relationship with price. Notice CBD, OCIMENE, TERPINOLENE, THCA, CBN all have non-zero relationships. Using this information could inform pricing changes in the future.

Looking at the same plots with Edibles, and seeing a definite structure in CBG, and possibly THCA, but not much else. CBG is interesting, a target for deeper dives in the future.

In the Vape product category we see that increased THC has a positive, linear relationship with price. This is the kind of generic bigger number means more money trend we expected to see more frequently, given the stereotypical population shopping at these stores.

Last is the same analysis with Carts, and we see a slight linear trend with THCA, and a distinct curved shape with CBD.
